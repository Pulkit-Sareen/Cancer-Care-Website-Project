Information for Patients

Learn about your specific type of cancer, its treatment options, and potential side effects. Ask questions and actively participate in discussions with your healthcare team. Seek reliable sources of information to understand your diagnosis and treatment plan.

Some Guidelines for Patients:

Build a Strong Healthcare Team

Build a healthcare team that you trust and feel comfortable with. This team typically includes oncologists, nurses, and other specialists. Establish open lines of communication and don't hesitate to seek a second opinion if needed.

Understand Your Diagnosis and Treatment

Take the time to understand your cancer diagnosis, treatment options, and potential side effects. Ask your healthcare team questions to ensure you have a clear understanding of your situation. This knowledge will empower you to make informed decisions about your treatment and care.

Communicate Openly

Share your feelings and concerns with your parents, caregivers, and healthcare team. Be honest about any discomfort, pain, or side effects you experience. Express your preferences and concerns regarding treatment decisions and supportive care

Take Care of Yourself

Follow your treatment plan, including medication schedules and recommended lifestyle changes. Practice good hygiene to prevent infections. Eat a balanced diet to support your overall health and recovery. Engage in physical activity suitable for your condition and energy levels.

Seek Support

Lean on your parents, caregivers, and friends for emotional support. Engage with local or online support organizations for children with cancer. Utilize available resources for financial assistance, educational support, and mental health services.
