Our Role in Cancer Research

At Child Cancer Care, our relentless pursuit is to advance cancer research, striving to discover a definitive cure, revolutionize early-stage detection methods, and safeguard the lives of innocent children affected by this devastating disease.

Overview on Cancer

Cancer is a major public health concern in India. India sees an incidence of more than 1 million new cases of cancer every year, a number that is likely to increase given the increasing age of Indian population and lifestyle changes. Cancer is enormously complex and highly adaptable; many subtypes of the disease have distinct clinical features and susceptibilities to therapy. Many cancers are still not diagnosed until they are at advanced stages and some resist most attempts at treatment.

Child Cancer Care has a clear definitive role to protect children from cancer and contribute in the research for the disease. Through our programs, multi-institutional networking programs, we have provided resources to individual researchers and associated several with government-backed instituitons, to carry out research in search for a break-through to develop new methods to prevent and treat disease and to conduct research especially in challenging areas pertaining to different types of cancer.

Projects we support

Child Cancer Care supports and contributes in various government-backed projects, as well as carry our own research projects.

Ayushman Bharat Health and Wellness Centres(AB-HWCs)

Screening of three common cancers i.e. oral, breast and cervical, along with other common Non-Communicable Diseases, is an integral part of service delivery under the AB-HWCs

Pradhan Mantri Jan ArogyaYojana (PMJAY)

Treatment of cancer under the three common cancers i.e. Pradhan Mantri Jan ArogyaYojana (PMJAY) (launched in 2018) has been one of the prime focus areas to safeguard the beneficiaries from catastrophic expenditure of cancer treatment. Health insurance cover of Rs. 5 lakhs per family per year is provided for secondary or tertiary care hospitalization to over 10.74 crore beneficiary families identified from SECC 2011 database. Chemotherapy and Radiotherapy4 packages, along with surgical oncology are covered as part of cancer treatment in the empanelled hospitals under the scheme. A total of 435 procedures have been defined for the treatment of cancer.

Strengthening Cancer Care Infrastructure

Cancer treatment facilities have also been created / planned in 13 State Government Medical Colleges which have been taken up for upgradation under PMSSY
